Pros and Cons For Different Seating Types
Sofa Sets/Sectional
If you entertain frequently, indulge in Sunday afternoon naps on your Sofa, or like sitting with your legs curled up, and space isn’t a constraint, you should opt for the super comfy L-shaped Sofa Sectionals or select from a combination of the coordinated 3, 2, 1 seater Tip: Just make sure that is it the RHS (right hand side ) or LHS (left hand side ) Sofa Sectional you require, so fgure out where exactly are you going to place it and decide which L shape part of your home you plan to utilize.
Sofa Recliners
Do you binge watch on TV series and look forward to stretching out in front of the screen on the weekends? You'll love our Recliners. Tip: Make sure there is enough room at the back and front of the Sofa, so either the manually-operated or electrical mechanism allows it open up completely and recline backwards or the front, giving you ample space to stretch your feet.
Sofa Cum Bed
Do you have frequent overnight guests who drop in often? Then the multi-functional Sofas, which magically transition on as seating by day and beds at night, are perfect for you. Tip: Just keep in mind that the full length of the Sofa when opened up to transform into a bed. Your room should have the requisite width and length to accommodate the fully-opened Sofa Bed. The next thing would be to contemplate which one will it be - the wood framed, the fully upholstered fabric, leatherette or metal one.

Consider The Attributes Of A Sofa
The Kind Of A Sit You Require
A higher seating height in a Sofa, provides the ease of getting up, whereas a lower seating is more casual and relaxed. Therefore, before making a decision, do keep in mind who is going to be using the Sofa - are they tall, short or older people? The seating height of our Sofas vary in sit – from the very low ones having a seating height of 8 inches to the taller variety going up to 24 inches. Though standard seating height is 18 inches.
The Width You Need
The width of a Sofa plays an important criteria in decision making too. You’ll need to measure the available space in your lounge to decide whether it is the 3, 2, 1 seater or large sectionals that your room needs.
